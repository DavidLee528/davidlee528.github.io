        <h1>Biography</h1>
        <br>
        <p>
            Tianhao Li is a thesis-track MSc candidate in Medical Physics at Duke University (DKU-based), where he is a member of Prof. <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=J6lv7y8AAAAJ" target="_blank">Fang-Fang Yin</a> and Prof. <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=oYqSQSkAAAAJ" target="_blank">Zhenyu Yang</a>'s research group. He also serves as a visiting researcher at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, under supervision of Prof. <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=Juoqtj8AAAAJ" target="_blank">Chaowei Xiao</a>. His primary research focus lies in <strong>Trustworthy and Responsible AI for Science (Healthcare)</strong>, exploring methods to ensure the reliability and ethical application of AI technologies in scientific domains.
        </p>
        <p>
            Tianhao holds a BEng in Information Security and leads the <em>Special Interest Group in Modern Interdisciplinary Research (SIGMIR)</em> (~20 members). He also serves as peer review experts for prestigious journals, conferences, and workshops, including AAAI, IJCAI, COLING, IJCNN, ISBI, JRESS, and ACM TIST. In addition, Tianhao serves as an individual contributor to the <a href="https://mlcommons.org/" target="_blank">MLCommons</a> AI Risk &amp; Reliability (AIRR) Working Group, contributing to the release of <a href="https://mlcommons.org/ailuminate/" target="_blank">AILuminate</a>. He also actively contributes to NVIDIA’s widely recognized open-source project <a href="https://github.com/NVIDIA/garak" target="_blank">NVIDIA/garak</a> (<strong>3.3K+ stars on Github</strong>).
        </p>
